Insects

The Insect Decline

46 high-confidence insect species are declining on the Front Range — more than any other group. Butterflies, moths, beetles, grasshoppers, and native bees all appear on the list.

Only one — Horace's Duskywing — has genuine independent corroboration: GBIF holds more records than iNaturalist for this species, confirming the decline through a truly separate data source.

Why so few confirmed? Colorado insect monitoring is almost entirely iNaturalist. GBIF aggregates iNaturalist data, so "corroboration" from GBIF often means seeing the same observations twice.

Methodology

How We Know This

Three layers of analysis, each filtering out noise from the one before.

  1. 1.iNaturalist baseline — 268,000+ research-grade observations. Observer participation up 5.2%, so species declines aren't explained by fewer people submitting data.
  2. 2.eBird cross-check — bird declines validated against Cornell Lab's dedicated birding database. 4 of 18 high-confidence bird declines confirmed.
  3. 3.RMNP comparison — declining species checked against Rocky Mountain National Park to separate urban habitat loss from regional climate signals.
